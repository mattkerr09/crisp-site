A practical waifu2x alternative for Mac

waifu2x is free, open source, offline, and on anime or illustration it still holds up. If it works for your footage, use it — no paid tool is worth money you don't need to spend. This page is about the specific places it stops working, because they're predictable.

What waifu2x was built for

It was trained on anime art: flat colour regions, clean line work, limited gradients. On that material it's excellent, and it remains one of the best free options for upscaling illustrations or animation stills. Nothing has really displaced it there.

The two places it breaks on real video

Photographic detail. Faces, skin texture, foliage, fabric — the model has no real training for reconstructing those, so it tends to smooth them into something plasticky. It isn't a bug; it's a model doing well on material it wasn't shown.

Temporal stability. This is the bigger one. waifu2x processes each frame independently, with no knowledge of the frames on either side. Small differences between consecutive frames get amplified differently, and the result shimmers during playback even though every individual frame looks fine paused.

That's why people say an upscale "looked great in screenshots and bad in motion." Judging on a paused frame hides the exact failure that matters most.

The practical friction

waifu2x is fundamentally an image tool. Upscaling video with it means extracting frames, processing thousands of images, then reassembling them with the audio — usually via ffmpeg, by hand. It works. It's also an evening, plus disk space for an uncompressed frame sequence, and one mistake in the frame numbering means starting over.

Honest recommendation

Upscaling anime, artwork or stills: stay on waifu2x. It's free, it's good, and paying for something else would be a waste.

Upscaling footage of the real world, especially anything that moves: the frame-independence problem won't go away with better settings, because it's structural. That's the point at which a video-native tool earns $19. Real-ESRGAN is the middle ground

Worth knowing: Real-ESRGAN handles photographic material far better than waifu2x and is also free. It has the same two practical catches — command-line only, and no temporal awareness — so it solves the detail problem while leaving the shimmer and the workflow untouched.

Judging an upscale properly

Whichever tool you land on, test it the same way. Take your worst clip, not a clean one — the whole point is difficult footage. Upscale a ten-second section rather than the whole thing, so you can iterate in minutes instead of hours.

Then check three specific things. Faces at 100% zoom, because over-smoothing shows there first. Fine repeating texture — brick, grass, knitted fabric — because that's what weak models turn into mush. And playback, at normal speed, watching for shimmer in flat areas like walls or sky. A tool that wins the first two and loses the third will disappoint you every time you actually publish something.

Cost is not the only price

Free tools cost time instead of money, and that's a real trade rather than a rhetorical one. An evening of frame extraction and ffmpeg reassembly is genuinely free if you enjoy it and genuinely expensive if you don't. Decide which kind of person you are before optimising for the $19.
