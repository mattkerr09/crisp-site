What HitPaw does better

Model selection, honestly. HitPaw exposes a set of models and names them after the job — a face model, an animation model, a general model — and lets you pick. When your footage is one clear category, hand-picking the right model beats an automatic choice, and animation in particular benefits because the correct model for line art is very different from the correct model for a human face.

Crisp decides for you. That is the right default for mixed real-world footage and it is one fewer thing to learn, but it is a real trade and pretending otherwise would be dishonest.

Where Crisp is the stronger pick

Two places. The first is that everything is local, without qualification — no feature quietly routes through a server, so there is no per-feature checking to do before you work with client footage. HitPaw's wider product line mixes local and cloud tools, and mixed is the hard case, because you have to know which is which.

The second is that Crisp edits. VikPea improves a file; you then open iMovie or Final Cut to assemble anything. Crisp lets you assemble the cut first and upscale the result once, at export, which is both less clicking and less GPU time spent on footage you cut out.

And the price gap is 5x for a similar core job. At $19 you can find out whether it works on your actual footage without the decision mattering much.

How to decide in ten minutes

Both have functional free tiers with watermarks, so run the same difficult clip through both — something with motion and a face, not a static shot, because static shots make every upscaler look good. Watch the results at full size, playing, not paused. Shimmer in flat areas is the failure mode that only appears in motion, and it is the axis worth judging on.

On lifetime licences in this category

Both of these are one-time purchases, and after Topaz moved to $299/year in October 2025 that is worth stating as a shared virtue rather than a differentiator. The category is drifting toward subscriptions because recurring revenue is easier to run a company on, not because upscaling got more expensive to compute. It runs on your own GPU either way.

What a lifetime licence does mean in practice is that updates eventually stop, or a major version becomes a new purchase. That is the honest catch with every perpetual licence including this one — the difference is that when updates stop on a local tool, the software you already have keeps working, which is not true of a cloud tool whose servers get switched off.

A note on animation and anime

If most of your footage is animation, take HitPaw's dedicated model seriously, and take waifu2x seriously too since it is free and was built for exactly this. Line art has hard flat-shaded edges that a model trained on photographic footage tends to soften into something slightly wrong. Crisp is tuned for real-world camera footage and does not pretend otherwise.
