How to upscale video on a Mac — free & offline

Turning a low-resolution, grainy, or old clip into clean 4K on a Mac used to mean a cloud service with per-minute fees, or a $299/year subscription. It doesn't anymore. Here's how to do it in about a minute — 100% on your Mac, no cloud, no watermark, free to try.

Updated July 2026 · step-by-step

The short version

  1. Download Crisp

    Grab the free Crisp app for Mac (Apple-Silicon native, notarized by Apple) and open it. Everything runs on your Mac — nothing is uploaded.

  2. Drop in your video

    Drag a low-res, grainy, compressed or old video onto the window. Crisp reads the file and checks its resolution automatically. JPEG/PNG/HEIC photos work too.

  3. Let Crisp choose — or pick a preset

    AUTO picks the right model and scale for you. Prefer control? Choose Enhance, Low-light, or Anime, and set the scale to 2×, 3× or 4× (up to 4K). Turn on Smooth motion to interpolate to a higher frame rate.

  4. Export

    Crisp upscales, denoises and sharpens the clip on-device and saves a clean new file beside your original — no watermark, no subscription, no waiting on an upload.

Tip: just describe what you want

Crisp has a plain-English box — type “upscale to 4K and clean up the low-light noise” or “make this vertical for TikTok” and it builds the plan for you. No wall of sliders to learn.

What kind of Mac do I need?

Any Apple-Silicon Mac (M1 or newer) runs the fast tier at interactive speed — 1080p to 4K in the time it takes to grab a coffee. The optional generative Max quality lane, for badly damaged footage, is happiest with 32 GB+ of memory and is the slow, best-quality, overnight option.
