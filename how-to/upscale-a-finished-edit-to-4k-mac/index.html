How to upscale a finished edit to 4K on a Mac

Cut your video on a real timeline, then upscale the whole finished edit to 4K in the same export — with the fast Enhance path or the generative SeedVR2 Max restore. All 100% offline: no Topaz cloud, no subscription.

Edit first, then upscale — in one job

Most tools make you choose: edit in one app, then upscale in another (usually a paid, cloud one like Topaz). Crisp does both. You cut your video on a real timeline, then tell Crisp to upscale the finished edit to 4K as part of the export — the whole thing runs offline on your Mac.

Step by step

  1. Build your edit on the timeline

    Open Crisp (it starts in the editor), import your clips, and arrange them on the timeline. Trim, split (press S), reorder, add photos, and give clips their own look or speed.

  2. Add a montage or music if you want

    Optionally combine clips into a montage or add a music track. Whatever you assemble is your finished cut.

  3. Choose Upscale on export

    In the Export panel, set Upscale on export to Enhance to 4K (fast) or Max — SeedVR2 remake (best). Off keeps the source resolution.

  4. Export

    Click Export video. Crisp renders your edit, then upscales the whole finished cut in the same job — entirely on your Mac. Enhance is fast; Max uses the generative SeedVR2 model and takes longer.

  5. Get your 4K file

    The upscaled edit is saved beside your project — clean 4K, no watermark, nothing uploaded.

Enhance vs Max

Enhance to 4K is the fast path for sharpening a whole edit quickly. Max is the generative SeedVR2 restore that rebuilds detail — slower. Both run on-device; Topaz Starlight is cloud-only.

Why this beats a separate upscaler

  • One pass, one app. No exporting a cut, re-importing into Topaz, paying per-minute in the cloud.
  • Offline and private. Your finished video never leaves your Mac.
  • One-time price. No yearly subscription just to upscale.

FAQ

Can I upscale a whole edited video, not just one clip?

Yes — build your edit on the timeline, set Upscale on export to Enhance or Max, and Crisp upscales the finished cut in the same export.

What is the difference between Enhance and Max?

Enhance is the fast upscale to 4K. Max is the generative SeedVR2 restore that rebuilds detail — higher quality, slower. Both are on-device.

Does it upload my video?

No. The whole edit-and-upscale runs on your Mac — nothing is uploaded, unlike Topaz cloud.

Upscale last, not first

Run the upscale on the finished edit rather than on each source clip. Upscaling every clip first means paying the render cost on footage you're about to trim away, and every subsequent transcode risks another generation of quality loss. Cut first, upscale once, export.
