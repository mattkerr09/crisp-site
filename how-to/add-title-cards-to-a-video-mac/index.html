Title cards, intros and outros

A title card is a still image — a title, a logo, or a photo — held on screen for a few seconds. Crisp lets you drop images straight onto the timeline alongside your video, so you can open with a title, label a section, or end on your logo, all in one offline edit.

Step by step

  1. Open Crisp and import your photo or title image

    Open Crisp (it starts in the editor) and Import the image you want as a title card — a photo, a logo, or a graphic you made. PNG, JPEG and HEIC all work.

  2. Add it to the timeline as a still

    Click Add as photo on the image in the Media panel. It drops onto the timeline as a held frame — place it at the start for an intro, at the end for an outro, or between clips.

  3. Set the duration

    Drag the card edge on the timeline (or edit its duration in the inspector) to hold it as long as you want — 2 to 3 seconds is typical for a title.

  4. Choose motion: Ken Burns or Hold

    A photo card gets a gentle Ken Burns zoom by default. For a clean logo or text card, select it and set Photo motion to Hold so it stays perfectly still.

  5. Add a transition and export

    Pick a transition like Dissolve so the card fades into your footage, then Export. Crisp renders the whole thing to a clean MP4 on your Mac.

Hold for logos, Ken Burns for photos

Photos look best with a subtle Ken Burns zoom (on by default). A text or logo card usually wants to stay still — select it and switch Photo motion to Hold. Mix both in one video.

Make a title image

Crisp holds any image you give it, so make your title card in whatever you like — Preview, Keynote, Canva, or a screenshot — export it as a PNG or JPEG, and drop it on the timeline. Want text over your footage? Use captions.

FAQ

How do I add a title card to a video on a Mac?

Import your title image, click Add as photo to place it on the timeline, set its duration, pick Hold or Ken Burns motion, add a transition, and export — all on your Mac.

How do I keep a logo card from zooming?

Select the card and set Photo motion to Hold. It stays perfectly still, ideal for logos and text.

Can I put text directly over my video?

For text on top of footage, use captions. Title cards are full-screen still images shown between or around your clips.
